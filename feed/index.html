]]> All About Veganism

Did you know the term vegan was coined by Donald Watson, a British animal rights activist in the 1940s? Moreover, it describes the philosophy that animals should not be exploited and hurt for food or other purposes.  Vegans do not consume animal products and foods. This includes meats and dairy, honey, leather etc.

Some of us wonder why anyone would not want to consume meat considering our Kenyan craze for “nyama choma”. Here’s a look into the vegan world and their choice of lifestyle.

Veganism is a widespread lifestyle practice these days that has been embraced largely for many reasons. For instance, the various health benefits, to protect animals and the environmental benefits that come with it.

Health benefits

Plant-based diets lower the chances of type 2 diabetes, reduce blood pressure levels, and increase metabolism. Moreover, it lowers cholesterol and ultimately reduces the chances of various cancers.

To protect Animals

About 150 million farmed animals are killed each day for profit. Overfishing, climate change and pollution have led to scientists expecting fishless oceans by the year 2048.  Individuals adhering to a vegan lifestyle advocate for the protection of animals.

Environmental benefits

A plant-based diet promotes a sustainable life that helps combat pollution, moreover it aids lowering emission of greenhouse gasses. Firstly, factory farming is responsible for greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and pollution.

  • Livestock and their byproducts are accountable for 51% of all greenhouse gas emissions, worldwide. 
  • 2,500 gallons of water are needed to produce 1 pound of beef. 
  • And animal agriculture is responsible for up to 91% of the Amazon rainforest’s destruction. 

In addition to their environmental and health benefits, a plant-based diet is delicious and diverse. It provides a large variety of options and tastes that would tempt anyone to adopt the lifestyle. Here’s a vegans guide to Mama-Ashanti’s plant-based options.

Delicious Mama Ashanti Plant-Based Options

1.KELEWELE – Hot spiced deep fried diced plantain.

This delicious and aromatic mouth-watering dish is a blend of ripe plantains, ginger, and spices that are cut into bite-sized pieces and fried to perfection. In addition to its incredible flavour, it is incredibly versatile.

It can be eaten as a snack, a side dish, or even as a main course with some accompaniments like rice or beans.

2. OKRA SOUP – Okra vegetable from West Africa cooked in Ashanti spices and red palm nut oil.

This hearty dish is made with fresh okra combined with a rich and flavorful broth along with a variety of aromatic spices and seasonings. Moreover, this combination creates a truly unique and delicious taste experience. The soup is hearty and filling, perfect for a chilly day or when you’re in need of some comfort food

With its vibrant green colour and beautiful presentation, Okra soup is as visually appealing as it is delicious.

3. EGUSI SOUP – Ground pumpkin seeds cooked together in spinach and West African spices in red palm nut oil.

Egusi soup is made with ground melon seeds, which are then simmered with a variety of flavorful herbs and spices. Furthermore, ground Egusi seeds give a unique flavour enhanced by the use of fragrant ingredients like onions, garlic, ginger, and chilli peppers.

The soup’s rich and creamy consistency is due to the addition of red palm nut oil, which gives it a beautiful golden colour and adds to its rich flavour profile. Whats more, this dish is not only delicious, but it is also highly nutritious.

4. JOLLOF RICE – Long grain rice cooked in spiced tomato sauce

This mouthwatering West African dish is sure to tantalize your taste buds. It is a delicious blend of long-grain rice, flavorful tomato sauce, aromatic spices, and a variety of vegetables. The rice is cooked to perfection and infused with the bold and savoury flavours of tomato sauce and spices. In addition to its unique taste, the dish is often prepared with vegetables like bell peppers and onions. Carrots are also commonly added to the mix, creating a colourful and nutritious feast.

Jollof rice is a truly comforting and satisfying dish that is perfect for any occasion.

5. FRIED YAM – Thick slices of West African-style fried yam, seasoned with spices and herbs

This dish is both crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Perfect as a delicious and satisfying snack or side dish.Moreover, what makes fried West African yam so appealing is its crispy texture, which is achieved by slicing the yam and frying them until they are golden brown.

The result is a satisfying crunch that gives way to the soft, velvety texture of the yam inside.

6. VEGETABLE RICE – Long grain rice fried with spring onion, cabbages, peas and carrots.

This all encompassing dish is sure to satisfy your cravings for a flavorful and healthy meal. In addition, it is made with a combination of fragrant rice, crisp and colourful vegetables, and a range of savoury seasonings and spices.This dish is the perfect balance of taste and nutrition.

It can be customised with a range of seasonings and spices, from mild and sweet to spicy and bold, making it the perfect choice for any taste preference.

7. COLESLAW SALAD – Shredded cabbage and carrots, onions, and bell peppers mixed with a creamy dressing.

What makes coleslaw so appealing is its perfect balance of flavours and textures. The vegetables provide a satisfying crunch and a subtle sweetness, while the dressing adds a tangy and creamy richness that ties everything together.

]]> If you’re looking for an authentic taste of West African dishes, Mama Ashanti is the perfect place to start. For first-time visitors, navigating the extensive menu can be a daunting task. Here’s a guide to some of the best dishes to try at our restaurant.

1. Jollof Rice

This popular West African dish is a must-try. The fragrant rice is cooked with a tomato-based sauce and seasoned with a blend of spices. That give it a unique flavor. Jollof Rice is often served with chicken or beef. But we offer vegetarian-friendly options to our customers. This West African classic

pairs extremely well with accompaniments Like buttered spinach, stemmed season vegetables or a side of fried plantains. 

2. Fufu

Fufu is a staple in West African cuisine, and Mama Ashanti’s version is not to be missed. This starchy dish is made by pounding boiled cassava and plantains until they become a smooth dough-like

consistency. It's often served with soup or stew. Preferably eaten with the fingers for a more joyous experience. 
3. Eforiro Soup 

Eforiro soup is a thick and hearty soup made with a variety of green leafy vegetables, meat or fish. And a combination of spices and seasonings. One of the unique characteristics of Eforiro soup is its thick and creamy texture. Which comes from the addition of ground peanuts. 

Eforiro soup is a delicious and nutritious dish that is well worth trying in our opinion. If you are looking to experience the flavors and culinary traditions of West Africa. Eforiro soup is definitely a dish to try. It’s perfect for any occasion. We recommend pairing our Eforiro soup with a side of rice, pounded yam, or fufu bringing about an unforgettable taste. 

4. Okra Soup

The irresistible Okra Soup should definitely be among your first go-to dish to try at our Lavington branch or our latest addition, Mama Ashanti Waterfront Karen Mall. It is a popular dish in West Africa that is known for its flavor and

texture. We prepare this nutritious soup using fresh okra, tomatoes, and onions, paired with either beef or fish. 
5. Egusi Soup

Egusi is a popular West African dish. Loved for its rich and creamy texture, as well as its delicious flavour. We prepare this dish using melon seeds. Which are ground into a fine paste and cooked with various spices for a delectable kick. Accompanied by tomatoes,

onions and vegetables. We prepare it with any goat, beef or fish but we do recommend it with our prime beef. 

In our restaurant, we recommend having the dish with a side of fufu or pounded yam. Which helps balance out the soup’s silky texture. Its unique qualities combined with a variety of nutritious ingredients, make it a staple of West African cuisine. Beloved by our customers. 

These are just a few of the many delicious dishes you can try at Mama Ashanti. Whether you’re a fan of spicy, savory, or sweet flavors. There’s something for everyone on the menu. Our restaurant also offers vegetarian and vegan options. So be sure to ask your server if you have any dietary restrictions. All our branches offer a welcoming atmosphere, perfect to experience the rich culinary traditions of West Africa.

]]> East meets West is a notion bringing out certain similarities between East African and West African cuisines. It seeks to highlight some of our similar cuisines, recipes and spices despite geographical and preferential differences.

One thing about Africans is our love for fresh, home-cooked meals. Thanks to our fertile lands, waterbodies and forests we have a variety of vegetables, fruits and meats provided to us by our beautiful land. We believe food should be shared with the whole community and everyone is welcome at our table. For this reason, it is rare to find an African house without food. Enough food is prepared just in case a guest shows up unexpectedly.

East Africa encompasses Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Burundi and even Rwanda. West Africa, on the other hand, involves Nigeria, Ghana, Togo, Gambia, Benin, Mali, and Sierra Leone among others. These two vast African regions share similarities in some cultures, traditional materials such as the kente and khanga, and food among many other things. However, our main focus is on delectable delicacies.

African cuisines present a variety of fruits, meats, nuts, spices, pulses, seeds and root tubers. Both regions cherish food. A variety of from different areas, with different names. They might have similar looks, slightly similar tastes, spices, or recipes and can be eaten with similar accompaniments.

Semolina and Ugali



Semolina is a West African meal prepared with durum wheat flour and butter. It is cooked just like Kenyan ugali which is made from maize flour by stirring the flour in hot water until it forms a thick, well-cooked paste. Both are eaten with various soups and stews, vegetables, and assorted meat dishes.

Jollof and Pilau


Pilau is a fragrant and spicy East African rice dish cooked with various spices, meat stock and rice. It is richly flavored and loved here in the East. Similarly, Jollof is a West African rice dish with tomatoes as the main ingredient. Prepared with long grain rice, spices, meats, and vegetables with different cooking methods depending on the West African country of origin.

Matoke(Bananas) and Dodo (Fried Plantain)


Matoke is a Ugandan cuisine prepared with green bananas. It is served in different ways, a favorite boiled whole and eaten with various meat stews, peanut sauce and vegetables. Contrastingly, Dodo is a West African delicacy prepared with fried sweet plantain. It can be eaten without stew as it is very sweet and can also act as a snack.

Peanut and Groundnut Sauce


Ugandan peanut sauce is prepared with minimal ingredients, peanuts are the center of attention. It is simmered for 2-3 minutes after blending the boiled peanuts then cooked in cooking oil and various spices. Nigerian groundnut sauce has a similar method of preparation, however, it utilizes a much larger variety of ingredients including various lumps of meat and their stocks. Eaten with rice, and sweet potatoes among other accompaniments.

Fish Dishes


Both regions enjoy fish stews, soups and deep-fried with kachumbari, especially in East Africa. West Africans include various fish in many of their recipes with assorted meats as compared to the East.

We have highlighted a few meals we deem similar despite there being a spectrum of shared delicacies and culture.

]]> Jollof Rice is the most popular and a favorite meal of the Of the West African countries. Known for its sweet aroma, deep red color and spiciness. How could they not clash over it? Nigeria’s Party Jollof and Ghana’s Jollof take the lead in the wars for the best Jollof rice recipes.

Jollof is a very popular rice dish, well beyond Africa, it has its own national day and festivals around the world. However, today we let go of its blooming reputation and dive deep into the ongoing Jollof Wars. In case you’re not aware , Let’s get you up to date! 

“Jollof rice wen dey carry last for inside pot, dey carry first for inside plate”

What are the Jollof Rice Wars?

The Jollof Rice Wars are the clashes on social media, mass media and in person across the world and different West African countries. Senegal, Nigeria and Ghana take the lead over who makes the best Jollof rice, in a bid to claim the Jollof rice throne.

The main consistent ingredients for preparation of Jollof is rice and tomatoes. Tomatoes for its deep-red color and rice , well, it is a rice dish. The other ingredients vary similar to how different armies employ different tactics during war to gain an upper hand over the opponents.

Nigerian Party Jollof with Deep Fried Chicken

The Senegalese Jollof, is prepared with broken rice , tomatoes, tomato paste ,onions, scotch bonnet peppers, salt, vegetable oil, fish, spices and fermented conch. Nigerians and Ghanaians ,however, played up the dish using their culinary prowess and  have since taken over as the key players in the wars. “The Jollof wars between these two players are more likely to do with the similarities between their Jollof rather than the differences. “stated Jiji Majiri Ugboma, a Nigerian food writer.

Nigerian Party Jollof or Ghanaian Spicy Jollof

Nigerian Party Jollof is prepared with long grain rice. Large particle parboiled rice ( precooked with husk) washed to change texture, boost nutrition and make it more resistant to weevils. It uses mild level tomato, sautéed onions in groundnut oil to build flavor. Thyme, pepper, bullion cubes, curry powder, chilies, paprika, red pepper spice for a warm and spicy sweetness , richness and color. Fish, preferably, crayfish, habanero pepper for a sweet and floral flavor. Nigerian Jollof rice applies a longer cooking process that produces a smoky note. This reacts with the tomato for a smoky, ashy and roasted tomato note giving a savory and delightful character to the rice.

On the contrary, Ghanaian Jollof has a higher tomato flavor composition since Ghanaians love tomatoes in its various forms. They also add meat to the tomato sauce. It has thus been credited to have the most tantalizing spices and flavors. The juiciest and richest meats with a bright orange/red color. Ghanaian Jollof is although not as spicy as Nigerian . It uses less chilies compared to Nigerian Party Jollof. It also prioritizes the use of tomato stew and meat stock for the rice unlike Nigerian Jollof. Uses ginger, onions and hot pepper. With a preference for a unique burnt taste from the reaction between the pot and rice for a sumptuous and authentic Jollof rice.

Although many, including the American Embassy in Ghana  have credited the Jollof win to Ghanaians . We simply cannot discredit the parallel superiority of the Nigerian Jollof. It would only be right to declare this a stalemate. Nonetheless , for every war ,there must be a winner and a loser.
